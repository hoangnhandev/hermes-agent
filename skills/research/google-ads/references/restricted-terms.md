# Google Ads Restricted Terms Reference

This document contains restricted terms and policy guidelines for Google Ads copy creation.

## Misleading Claims (Prohibited)

### Absolute Claims
- guaranteed
- guarantee
- #1
- number one
- best ever
- top rated (without verification)
- most popular
- leading (without verification)
- premier (without verification)

### Performance Claims
- instant results
- immediate results
- overnight results
- miracle
- magical
- revolutionary
- breakthrough
- unprecedented

### Financial Claims
- get rich quick
- free money
- guaranteed income
- guaranteed profit
- specific returns (e.g., "1000% return")
- risk-free investment

## Healthcare Claims (Prohibited for Non-Certified Businesses)

### Treatment Claims
- cure
- treat
- prevent
- heal
- remedy
- therapeutic
- medical treatment
- diagnosis

### Health Outcomes
- guarantees health outcomes
- promises specific health improvements
- claims about diseases or conditions

## Personal Attributes (Do Not Target)

### Protected Characteristics
- gender-based targeting (e.g., "for women only", "men's service")
- age-based targeting (e.g., "senior discount", "youth service")
- race or ethnicity targeting
- religious affiliation targeting
- sexual orientation targeting
- disability targeting

## Character Limits

### Responsive Search Ads
- **Headlines**: Maximum 30 characters each
- **Descriptions**: Maximum 90 characters each
- **Paths**: Maximum 15 characters each
- **Final URLs**: No character limit but must be valid URLs

### Best Practices
- Use all available headline slots (3-15)
- Use all available description slots (2-4)
- Keep paths relevant and concise
- Ensure URLs are functional and lead to relevant content

## Conditional Terms (Review Required)

These terms may be acceptable if factually true:

- free (must actually be free)
- no cost (must actually be no cost)
- complimentary (must actually be complimentary)
- discount (must be genuine discount)
- sale (must be genuine sale)
- limited time (must be genuinely limited)
- exclusive (must be genuinely exclusive)

## Prohibited Content Types

### Contact Information
- Phone numbers in headlines or descriptions
- Email addresses in headlines or descriptions
- Physical addresses in headlines or descriptions

### Excessive Punctuation
- More than 2 exclamation points (!) in a single ad
- More than 2 question marks (?) in a single ad
- ALL CAPS for more than 3 consecutive characters

### Triggering Terms
- 100% (unless referring to a verifiable statistic)
- always
- never
- everyone
- all
- every

## Geographic Targeting Guidelines

### Location References
- Only include locations where you actually operate
- Don't imply nationwide service if local only
- Use specific city names, not vague terms like "local area"

### Service Area Claims
- Be honest about service coverage
- Don't claim to serve areas you don't serve
- Use "near me" only if you have actual local presence

## Industry-Specific Restrictions

### Legal Services
- No guarantees of case outcomes
- No promises of specific results
- Must include attorney licensing information if applicable

### Financial Services
- No guaranteed returns
- No specific performance promises
- Must include regulatory disclaimers if applicable

### Medical Services
- No promises of cures or treatments
- Must include proper disclaimers
- Must have proper certifications if making medical claims

## Enforcement and Consequences

### Policy Violations
- Ads may be disapproved
- Account may be suspended for repeated violations
- Poor account health affects ad delivery

### Appeal Process
- Disapproved ads can be appealed through Google Ads interface
- Provide documentation to support claims when appealing
- Fix violations before resubmitting

## Best Practices for Compliance

1. **Be Honest**: Only make claims you can substantiate
2. **Be Specific**: Use specific, verifiable information
3. **Be Clear**: Avoid vague or misleading language
4. **Be Current**: Stay updated on policy changes
5. **Be Local**: Only target areas you actually serve

## Regular Updates

Google Ads policies change regularly. This document should be reviewed and updated:
- Monthly for policy changes
- When creating campaigns in new industries
- Before launching major new campaigns
